# Unit Economics and Growth Math

## Baseline assumptions

- Monthly top-of-funnel audits: 1,200
- Audit completion rate: 42% -> 504 completed audits
- Lead conversion from completed audits: 24% -> 121 leads
- Qualified lead rate: 45% -> 54 qualified leads
- Sales call booking rate from qualified leads: 35% -> 19 calls
- Close rate from calls: 22% -> 4.2 new paying customers/month

## CAC assumptions

- Community + founder content spend: $1,500/month
- Outbound tooling + ops spend: $1,000/month
- Founder/operator time cost (allocated): $2,000/month
- Total acquisition cost: $4,500/month
- Estimated CAC: $4,500 / 4.2 = **$1,071 per customer**

## Revenue assumptions

- Productized audit + ongoing optimization service: $600 MRR average contract
- New MRR/month from 4.2 customers: 4.2 * $600 = **$2,520**
- 3-month average retention scenario:
  - LTV ~ $1,800 per customer (ignoring expansion)
- LTV:CAC baseline: 1.68x (needs improvement in early phase)

## Profitability logic (MVP stage)

- Primary objective is lead quality and repeatable demand, not immediate profit.
- Improve economics by:
  - raising close rate from 22% to 30%
  - increasing ACV through team/portfolio bundles
  - reducing paid outreach share as referral loop improves
- At close rate 30% and $750 MRR contracts:
  - expected new customers/month ~5.7
  - expected new MRR/month ~ $4,275
  - CAC payback drops below 2.5 months.

## Pathway to $1M ARR

- Target MRR for $1M ARR: **$83,333**
- Scenario:
  - 180 active customers
  - average MRR = $465
  - ARR = 180 * 465 * 12 = $1,004,400
- Practical path:
  - Month 1-3: validate conversion + pricing + recommendation trust
  - Month 4-8: channel scale via communities + partner agencies
  - Month 9-12: expansion revenue from quarterly optimization and multi-team accounts
