# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact target user

- Engineering manager, head of product, or founder at a 8-80 person startup.
- Team is already paying for 2+ AI tools and suspects seat overlap.
- Buying urgency: monthly burn reduction before next fundraising milestone.

## Acquisition channels (initial)

- Founder and engineering communities:
  - r/startups
  - Indie Hackers
  - YC Startup School forum
  - SaaS CTO/VP Engineering Slack groups
- Operator-led LinkedIn posts with before/after spend examples.
- Cold outbound to recently funded startups with tailored spend benchmark snippets.

## First 100 users strategy

- Run 20 manual "white-glove" audits in founder communities to calibrate recommendations.
- Publish 5 anonymized teardown posts ("How a 12-person team saved $X/month").
- Offer a free "AI spend sanity check" teardown for teams that share their stack profile.
- Partner with 3 boutique dev agencies to audit their client portfolios.

## Distribution strategy

- Build a reusable share loop:
  - user completes audit
  - gets a public share page
  - shares internally to finance/cofounder
- Add lightweight referral CTA on result pages:
  - "Run this for another team in your portfolio."
- Turn insights into distribution assets:
  - short clips
  - spend benchmark cards
  - practical migration checklists

## Traction expectations (first 8 weeks)

- Week 1-2: 40-60 audits from communities and founder network.
- Week 3-4: 120+ cumulative audits with first repeat teams.
- Week 5-8: 250+ cumulative audits, 40-60 qualified leads, 8-12 pilot calls.
